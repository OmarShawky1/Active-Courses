# Notes
1. Tags meaning:
	1. Resources Hierarchy (The highest tag is the best) :
		1. #Ordered: Resources are ordered based on the explanation quality. 
		2. #Tested
		3. #Interactive: Courses provide practice.
		4. #Recommended
		5. #Crashcourse: Very short video to provide minimal acceptable knowledge about a topic.
		6. #Post: A Facebook or LinkedIn post that includes a lot of information/resources/advice about a specific Topic in [[MAP]], yet I do not need to extract all the information from it currently, but I will do so when time comes (i.e., I will need it). (can be used as a hashtag or if i cloned the post, I shall write that it is a post)
	2. #NACRW: Not all courses are written.
		4. Reasons for not writing all courses:
			1. Have very low number of views.
			2. Not relative to me.
	3. #Freemium: Partially requires to pay.
	4. #Low-Premium: At a very feasible price.
	5. #Premium: Requires to pay.
	6. #Not_English: Some contents are not in English and instead of writing each content's language, it is better just to point it out that way. (Most of Non-English Contents are actually Arabic as it is my mother language)
2. Update tags.
	1. Cases:
		1. If you found out that it is more general or more narrow.
		2. If you found out that the resource fulfills one of the aforementioned tags condition.
	2. Explanation: 
		1. Some links have very general or narrow tags although they are not but I didn't know at that time I was adding the tag. So, in case such scenario happen, Update the tag.
4. Considerations:
	1. A resource might have multiple videos or contents to explain the same thing, so do not cling/stop on the first resource that you face. 
	2. Links might get updated but the written contents are not; So, consider re-searching within the links again.
	3. Some contents (especially GitHub Repositories) are old; So, take care before you use an old information.
		1. Non-science contents (E.g., Technology) that are 3 years old or more are not mentioned and should not be mentioned because they change over time.
		2. Some Non-science contents doesn't differ that much (E.g., Git); So, Treat it with grain of salt.
